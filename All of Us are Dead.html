Train to Busan...

By Skie Degale



It was a normal day. A bit cloudy, but what could you expect from Korea? It all started from when I was at work. Busy day I must say, getting calls from left and right just when my accountant was on holiday. One of my most anno-... I mean ambitious co-workers, Ho-sang, was nagging me about colosing a finance in the start of the fiscal year. After justifying the matter with a hard no, I went on to the more important matters at hand.

On-jo, my daugther. Her birthday passed a day ago and I wanted to get her an awesome gift. But what would I get her? I asked my coworker before he left and he recomemned a tv remote. Why did I think it was a good idea at the time? No Idea. And apparently, I had already bought one for her the year before. As I arrived home, my mother, Hu-san, was wating for me at the door. She began telling about On-jo biggest birthday wish, to see her mother again; and no we are not together anymore. I brushed her off, and as I went to her bedroom, I saw On-jo on a calll with her mother, speaking as low as possible as if she did not want mo to hear her on a call with her mom. I entered the room as she quietly told her mom goodbye, that I was here to talk to her.

With the sweetest but a little bit sacaratic simle on her face, she asked me. "Ba-ba, why can't I go to see mother?" I said, "You know I have work. But enough of this, I have a gi- " "I can go by myself, just drop me off at the train station." "Honey, I love you, but you cannot go to Busan right now. I know you want to see her, but it is not a good time right now." "When will be a good time?" I paused. "...Lets just forget about this, here open your present. :)"

As she opens it, I see the defeat on her face, though she tries to hide it as she says. "Thanks ba-ba, but you already got this for me twice." *Apparently I got it for her twice* But as any good parent would want to see their child happy, I sighed and said. "Pack your things, I am taking you to see your mom." She was so exicted that she gave me a tight hug and went to go call her mom.

I exited the room, my mother told me she was proud of me for making such a big decision. I scuffed, saying that it was the only way to make her happy, and I did not want to lose the bond I had with my only daugter. That night, I questioned the decisision I made. Was I doing the right thing? Oh well, there is no turning back now, tommmorow is a new day, and we are on our way, to the Train to Busan.


On-jo and I were in the car, she was looking out the window, barely talking to me, when suddenly, white dust-like particles began to fall. As she caught one in her lap, police, firearms, and firefighter trucks began speeding through, head to what seem slike a drastic event. But not to worry, we'll be fine....right? As we were running to the train, numerous people were bombarding the doors over what seemed like from afar a video star. We got on the train, and we found our seats, On-jo sat by the window, I sat by the isle. The train took off, I did not notice that people outside were ruuning away from...something.
